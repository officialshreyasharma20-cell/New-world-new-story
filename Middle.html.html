Middle: The Forest That Remembers

Kael’s dragon carried them beyond Skyhaven and into the wild sky, where floating islands became fewer and the clouds below turned dark purple. Lyra held tightly to the saddle, while Ori clung to her backpack and complained about wind pressure with great scientific seriousness.

They landed at the edge of the Crystal Forest of Echoes. The trees were transparent and impossibly tall, their branches filled with glowing memories. Some showed children laughing in Skyhaven’s markets. Others showed wars, storms, and cities falling into the clouds.

“Do not touch the trees,” Kael warned. “They show truth, but not always kindly.”

The forest path opened only when Ori stepped forward. Blue symbols lit across his brass body, and a forgotten door appeared between two crystal trunks. Inside the door was a puzzle made of rotating star-rings. Each ring showed a different age of Aetheria.

Ori’s eyes widened. “I remember this. The guardians built the Beacon after the Void King tried to swallow the sky. But they did not destroy him. They removed his name from the memory of the world.”

Then the shadows attacked. They rose from between the crystal roots, tall and faceless, with hands like smoke. Kael drew his enchanted sword, and blue fire ran along the blade. Lyra threw her crystal device into the air, creating a shield of spinning gears and light.

One tree showed Lyra a future where she returned to Skyhaven as a hero, but Kael and Ori were gone. Another showed Ori restored as a perfect guardian, but without his new personality. A third showed Kael standing before the Celestial Beacon years ago, opening its sealed gate for a stranger in a dark crown.

“You helped awaken the Beacon before,” Lyra said.

Kael lowered his sword. “I was tricked. I thought I was guiding scholars. I did not know they served the Void King.”

Ori placed both hands on the star-rings. “I know the final route, but unlocking it will restore the memory they stole from me.”

“Is that bad?” Lyra asked.

Ori’s voice became small. “I may remember fear.”

Lyra knelt beside him. “Then we will remember it with you.”

Ori turned the final ring. The forest erupted in blue light. His lost memories returned: the guardians, the first battle, the Beacon, the Void King’s real name sealed inside a crystal key.

He also remembered the terrible truth. To stop the Void King forever, someone would have to give up their most precious memory at the heart of the Beacon.

Kael looked toward the horizon. There, beyond the forest, the Celestial Beacon rose above the clouds, its lens burning like a second moon.

“Then we go,” Lyra said.

Ori climbed onto her shoulder. “For the record, I am afraid.”

Lyra smiled sadly. “So am I.”
